## 1. Executive Summary

**Name:** IntentBid
**Tagline:** The AI proposal engineer built to win.
**One-Liner:** IntentBid transforms traditional, manual proposal writing into high-velocity persuasion engineering using a proven 6-layer intent framework. 
**Target Audience:** Enterprise capture teams, bid managers, VP of Sales, Federal Consulting Firms, Mid-Market Defense Contractors.

## 2. The Core Narrative

### The Old Way vs. IntentBid
*   **The Old Way:** Generic templates, copy-paste errors, slow timelines (2-4 weeks), and inconsistent persuasion tied strictly to individual headcount or "tribal knowledge."
*   **The IntentBid Way:** Organizational memory that compounds, hours instead of weeks, tailored persuasion tailored to evaluator criteria, and 10x proposal velocity.

### The 6-Layer Persuasion Engine (The "Intent Framework")
Whenever talking about the product’s core IP, always refer to the **6 Layers**:
1.  **Brand Voice:** Your tone and terminology in every word.
2.  **Section Best Practices:** Proven structure for each section type.
3.  **Persuasion Frameworks:** AIDA, PAS, FAB, STAR — calibrated per section.
4.  **Win Themes:** Your differentiators woven throughout.
5.  **Competitive Positioning:** Indirect framing that elevates you.
6.  **Evidence & Context:** Claims backed by your actual capabilities.

## 4. Visual Identity (Color Palette)

The IntentBid color scheme is built on a dark, obsidian foundation (Zinc 900/950) punctuated by vivid, energetic neon gradients (Indigo, Purple, Pink). It evokes the feeling of "GitHub Copilot meets Enterprise Proposal Software."

### Backgrounds (The Void)
*   **Obsidian / Zinc 950:** `#09090b` (Primary Background)
*   **Graphite / Zinc 900:** `#18181b` (Secondary Cards / Modals)
*   **Deep Space / Zinc 800:** `#27272a` (Borders / Sub-surfaces)

### Core Gradients & Accents (The Spark)
*   **Indigo 400:** `#818cf8`
*   **Purple 400:** `#c084fc`
*   **Pink 400:** `#f472b6`
*   *Usage:* Gradients should typically run from Indigo -> Purple -> Pink left-to-right or diagonally.

### Validation & Success (The Human Loop)
*   **Emerald 400:** `#34d399`
*   *Usage:* Used sparingly to denote successful processing, human validation, or a "passing" compliance score.

### Typography
*   **Primary Typeface:** `Inter` (or similar clean sans-serif like SF Pro / Geist).
*   **Code / App Typeface:** Monospace (e.g., JetBrains Mono) for UI elements, "processing" terminal readouts, and file names.
*   **Weights:** 300 (Light) for body text, 400 (Regular) for standard UI, 700 (Bold) for subheadings, 800/900 (Extra/Black) for massive hero text.

## 6. Glossary & Key Terms

*   **Human-in-the-loop:** The core philosophy that AI drafts, but humans validate. 
*   **L1 / Company Truth:** The canonical data of the company (case studies, past performance).
