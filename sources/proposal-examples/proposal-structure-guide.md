## Standard Proposal Structure

Based on industry research and available Capgemini materials, a typical cloud migration/modernization proposal follows this structure:

---

## 1. Executive Summary (1-2 pages)

### Purpose
Outcome-first framing of the entire proposal. Should answer: "What will the client achieve?"

### Key Elements
- Client's current challenge (brief)
- Desired outcomes and business impact
- Capgemini's approach summary
- Key differentiators
- Investment overview
- Timeline highlights

### Writing Guidelines
- Lead with outcomes, not capabilities
- Use client's language and priorities
- Include 2-3 compelling metrics
- Keep under 2 pages

---

## 2. Understanding of Requirements (3-5 pages)

### Purpose
Demonstrate deep understanding of client's situation and prove we've listened.

### Key Elements
- Current state assessment
- Pain points and challenges
- Business drivers
- Technical constraints
- Regulatory/compliance requirements
- Success criteria from client's perspective

### Writing Guidelines
- Mirror client's RFP language
- Show industry expertise
- Reference similar situations we've addressed
- Validate understanding without repetition

---

## 3. Proposed Solution / Approach (5-10 pages)

### Purpose
Detail HOW Capgemini will achieve the client's outcomes.

### Key Elements

#### 3.1 Solution Overview
- High-level approach
- Key principles
- Alignment to client objectives

#### 3.2 Methodology
- Phase breakdown
- Key activities per phase
- Deliverables per phase
- Quality gates

#### 3.3 Technology Approach
- Platforms and tools
- Architecture principles
- Integration approach
- Security framework

#### 3.4 Governance
- Project management approach
- Communication cadence
- Escalation paths
- Risk management

### Writing Guidelines
- Connect every element back to client outcomes
- Be specific about deliverables
- Include visual diagrams
- Reference methodology (CCMF, ADMnext, etc.)

---

## 4. Team and Resources (2-4 pages)

### Purpose
Build confidence in delivery capability through people.

### Key Elements
- Key personnel with relevant experience
- Team structure and roles
- Onshore/offshore model
- Resource ramp-up plan
- Training and knowledge transfer

### Writing Guidelines
- Highlight relevant experience
- Include brief bios for key staff
- Show commitment (named resources)
- Address client's delivery model preferences

---

## 5. Case Studies / References (2-4 pages)

### Purpose
Prove we've done this before with measurable results.

### Key Elements
- 2-3 relevant case studies
- Quantified outcomes
- Industry/challenge similarity
- Lessons learned applied

### Format per Case Study
```
Client: [Industry, size, geography]
Challenge: [Similar to prospect's situation]
Solution: [What we delivered]
Results: [Specific metrics]
Relevance: [Why this matters to this proposal]
```

### Writing Guidelines
- Choose cases that match client's industry or challenge
- Lead with outcomes, not activities
- Include specific metrics (%, $, time)
- Get permission/anonymize as needed

---

## 6. Project Plan / Timeline (2-3 pages)

### Purpose
Show clear path from kickoff to outcomes.

### Key Elements
- Phase timeline
- Key milestones
- Dependencies
- Go-live targets
- Post-implementation support

### Format
- Gantt chart or visual timeline
- Phase descriptions
- Milestone definitions
- Assumptions clearly stated

---

## 7. Investment / Pricing (2-5 pages)

### Purpose
Transparent, justified pricing aligned to value delivered.

### Key Elements

#### 7.1 Pricing Summary
- Total investment
- Phase breakdown
- Payment schedule

#### 7.2 Pricing Model
- Fixed price components
- T&M components
- Rate card (if applicable)
- Assumptions

#### 7.3 Value Justification
- ROI calculation
- Cost savings projections
- Risk mitigation value
- Speed-to-value benefits

### Writing Guidelines
- Tie costs to outcomes
- Be transparent about assumptions
- Offer options if appropriate
- Include what's in/out of scope

---

## 8. Terms and Conditions (as required)

### Key Elements
- Contract type (MSA, SOW structure)
- Payment terms
- Intellectual property
- Warranties
- Liability limitations
- Change management process

---

## 9. Appendices

### Common Appendices
- Detailed resumes
- Certifications and accreditations
- Detailed technical architecture
- Tool descriptions
- Glossary
- Response to specific RFP requirements

---

## Proposal Format Guidelines

### Length
- Letter proposals: 10-20 pages
- Full proposals: 30-80 pages
- RFP responses: Per RFP requirements

### Design
- Professional, clean layout
- Consistent branding
- Executive summary accessible
- Visual elements (diagrams, charts)
- Clear section navigation

### Tone
- Client-focused (not capability-focused)
- Outcome-oriented
- Confident but not arrogant
- Specific and concrete

---

## Quality Checklist

Before submission, verify:

- [ ] Every claim is verifiable against L1 evidence
- [ ] Every section serves at least one stated outcome
- [ ] All RFP requirements addressed
- [ ] Pricing matches scope
- [ ] Team availability confirmed
- [ ] Case studies permission obtained
- [ ] Legal review completed
- [ ] Executive sign-off obtained
